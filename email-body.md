# From Data to Demand: A Community-Owned Platform for Health Equity Monitoring Across Kenya's 47 Counties

*Gerald Kombo - Independent Researcher and Developer, Kenya Health Equity Map*

## Sub-theme 2: Digital Health and Evidence Generation Through Community-Led Monitoring

**Introduction:** Community-Led Monitoring needs evidence people can use. In Kenya, national averages hide what is happening on the ground. The C-section rate of 17% looks reasonable until you see that 34% of births among affluent households are surgical while women in Mandera and Turkana deliver at home without a skilled attendant.

**Description of intervention:** The Kenya Health Equity Map turns public data into a simple score anyone can verify with pen and paper. The Priority Gap Score combines three factors: travel time to the nearest clinic, poverty rate, and population per facility. The platform works fully offline on a basic smartphone after one load. No login, no data collection, no installation. Users tap a county, view the score, and can print a one-page brief with source citations.

**Findings and lessons learned:** Turkana scores 92 out of 100 (42.6% poverty, 47% home births). Nairobi scores 40 (16.5% poverty, 618 facilities). The gap between them is not abstract. It is a woman in Turkana walking 90 minutes to a clinic while a woman in Nairobi has one within walking distance. Communities in Turkana, Tana River (PGS 89, 72.5% poverty), and Elgeyo-Marakwet cross-checked the data against local knowledge and added missing clinics to OpenStreetMap themselves.

**Conclusion and next steps:** A community can now bring a printed brief to a County Health Management Team meeting and ask: "Turkana scored 92 last year. Has resource allocation closed this gap?" The platform runs at zero cost. No server, no license, no donor funding required. Any community can use it, adapt it, or build on it.
