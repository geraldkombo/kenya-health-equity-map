# From Data to Demand: A Community-Owned Platform for Health Equity Monitoring Across Kenya's 47 Counties

*Gerald Kombo - Independent Researcher and Developer, Kenya Health Equity Map*

## Sub-theme 2: Digital Health and Evidence Generation Through Community-Led Monitoring

**Introduction:** Kenya's national averages hide local crises. Skilled birth attendance is 89% nationally but only 50% in Mandera and 53% in Turkana, where nearly half of births happen at home. Under-5 mortality exceeds 70 per 1,000 in Homa Bay and Turkana - nearly five times the rate in Nyeri. The Kenya Health Equity Map translates hidden data into a Priority Gap Score (0-100) that communities own.

**Description of intervention:** The tool eliminates gatekeepers. No permission, no login, no data collection, no internet after a single load. A community health worker opens it on a basic phone in airplane mode and views a one-page evidence brief showing where their health system is failing.

**Findings and lessons learned:** Turkana scores 92 out of 100 - a severe gap where a mother travels 90 minutes to a clinic, 47% of births happen at home, and 42.6% live in poverty. Nairobi scores 40, with 618 mapped clinics and 16.5% poverty. Communities in Turkana, Tana River (score 89, 72.5% poverty), and Elgeyo-Marakwet ground-truthed these numbers and added missing clinics to OpenStreetMap.

**Conclusion and next steps:** Citizens with this offline evidence can attend legally mandated County Health Management Team meetings (Kenya Health Act 2017) and ask: "Last year Turkana scored 92 - mothers faced 90-minute journeys to clinics. What has changed?" No intermediaries. No NGO. Just citizens with data that cost nothing and belongs to no one but them.
