From Data to Demand: A Community-Owned Platform for Health Equity Monitoring Across Kenya's 47 Counties

**Body of the abstract (must not exceed 300 words):**

**Introduction:** Community-led monitoring requires evidence communities can own and act upon. Kenya's national health averages obscure severe county-level disparities in access and outcomes. The national C-section rate of 17% masks a divide in which 34% of births among higher-income urban women are surgical, while women in Mandera and Turkana deliver at home without skilled attendance.

**Description of intervention:** The Kenya Health Equity Map is an offline-first digital platform that transforms health, demographic, and infrastructure data into actionable evidence. It employs the Priority Gap Score (PGS), a transparent 0-100 metric combining travel time to the nearest clinic, poverty rates, and population pressure on facilities, published as a verifiable calculation any community member can audit. Users navigate an interactive map, generate printable source-cited county briefs, and compare any two counties side-by-side. The platform requires no login, collects no data, and functions fully offline after a single load.

**Findings and lessons learned:** Turkana scores 92 (highest nationally, 47% home births, 42.6% hardcore poverty) against Nairobi at 40 (618 facilities, 16.5% poverty). The platform demonstrates that complex access barriers translate into straightforward advocacy tools. Communities in Turkana, Tana River (72.5% poverty, PGS 89), and Elgeyo-Marakwet validated the data and contributed missing facility coordinates to OpenStreetMap, strengthening the evidence base that informs their own advocacy.

**Conclusion and next steps:** Equipping communities with printable evidence and a trackable, verifiable score strengthens Community-Led Monitoring outcomes beyond process reporting. Communities can demand accountability: "Turkana scored 92 last year. Has resource allocation closed this gap?" The platform operates at zero ongoing cost, independent of donor funding cycles, and remains freely available for any community to adopt or adapt.

**Keywords:** Digital Health, Community-Led Monitoring, Health Equity, Civic Technology, Kenya
