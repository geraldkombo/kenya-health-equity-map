From Data to Demand: A Community-Owned Platform for Health Equity Monitoring Across Kenya's 47 Counties

**Body of the abstract (must not exceed 300 words):**

**Introduction:** Community-led monitoring needs evidence communities can own and act on. Kenya's national health averages mask severe county-level disparities. The C-section rate of 17% conceals a divide where 34% of births among higher-income urban women are surgical, while women in Mandera and Turkana deliver at home without skilled attendance.

**Description of intervention:** The Kenya Health Equity Map is an offline-first digital platform that translates health, demographic, and infrastructure data into visual evidence. It uses the Priority Gap Score (PGS), a transparent 0-100 measure combining travel time to clinic, poverty rates, and population pressure on facilities, published as a verifiable paper calculation. Users explore an interactive map, generate printable county briefs, and compare any two counties side-by-side. It requires no login, collects no data, and works offline after one load.

**Findings and lessons learned:** Turkana scores 92 (highest nationally, 47% home births, 42.6% hardcore poverty) against Nairobi at 40 (618 facilities, 16.5% poverty). The platform proves complex access barriers translate into simple advocacy tools. Communities in Turkana, Tana River (72.5% poverty, PGS 89), and Elgeyo-Marakwet validated the data and contributed missing facility coordinates to OpenStreetMap, expanding the evidence base communities themselves own.

**Conclusion and next steps:** Equipping communities with printable evidence and a trackable score strengthens CLM outcomes. A community can now ask: "Turkana scored 92 last year - has resource allocation closed this gap?" The platform operates at zero ongoing cost, independently of donor cycles, freely available for any community to use.

**Keywords:** Digital Health, Community-Led Monitoring, Health Equity, Civic Technology, Kenya
