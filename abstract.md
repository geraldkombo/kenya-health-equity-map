From Data to Demand: A Community-Owned Platform for Health Equity Monitoring Across Kenya's 47 Counties

**Body of the abstract (must not exceed 300 words):**

**Introduction:** Effective Community-Led Monitoring (CLM) requires accessible evidence to advocate for health equity. In Kenya, profound disparities in healthcare infrastructure often lack transparent, community-owned visualization tools. The national C-section rate of 17% conceals a divide where 34% of births among affluent households are surgical, while women in marginalized counties deliver at home.

**Description of intervention:** The Kenya Health Equity Map is an offline-first digital platform translating complex spatial data into a Priority Gap Score (PGS), a verifiable paper calculation combining travel time to the nearest clinic, poverty rates, and population pressure. Designed for digital inclusion, it operates fully offline on basic smartphones without logins or data tracking. Users navigate an interactive map, compare counties, and generate printable, source-cited evidence briefs.

**Findings and lessons learned:** The platform reveals severe disparities: Turkana scores 92 (highest nationally; 47% home births, 42.6% poverty) against Nairobi at 40 (618 facilities, 16.5% poverty). Complex access barriers successfully translate into straightforward advocacy tools. Community health teams in Turkana, Tana River (72.5% poverty, PGS 89), and Elgeyo-Marakwet validated the data and contributed missing facility coordinates to OpenStreetMap, asserting data sovereignty and strengthening the evidence base.

**Conclusion and next steps:** Equipping communities with printable evidence and a trackable score strengthens CLM outcomes beyond process reporting, enabling tangible demands for accountability: "Turkana scored 92 last year; has resource allocation closed this gap?" Operating on zero-cost infrastructure, the platform outlasts donor funding cycles and remains freely available for any community to adapt.

**Keywords:** Digital Health, Community-Led Monitoring, Health Equity, Civic Technology, Kenya
