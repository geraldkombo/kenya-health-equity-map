Dear Selection Committee,

I am pleased to submit the **Kenya Health Equity Map (KHEM)** for the 2026 Kenneth C. Frazier Award for Maternal Health Equity.

Every day, women in rural Kenya face a triple barrier to safe childbirth: they live hours from the nearest facility with a skilled birth attendant, they cannot afford the transport cost to reach it, and the facilities that do exist are overwhelmed by demand. These barriers have long been documented in separate datasets — travel time studies, poverty surveys, facility inventories — but never integrated into a single tool accessible to the communities and county planners who need it most.

KHEM solves this. It is an **open-source, privacy-preserving digital platform** that combines travel time, poverty, facility density, and skilled birth attendance (SBA) data into a single, publicly accessible map of all 47 Kenyan counties. Its core innovation — the **Priority Gap Score (PGS)** — identifies at a glance which counties face the most severe compounded barriers, enabling evidence-based advocacy for mobile maternal clinics, ambulance services, and skilled birth attendant deployment.

The platform already includes SBA rates from the Kenya Demographic and Health Survey (KDHS 2022) alongside travel time estimates from WHO AccessMod. Users can identify maternal health access deserts, compare neighbouring counties side-by-side, and generate printable advocacy briefs for direct use in County Health Management Team planning meetings. It requires no login, no tracking, and no cookies — designed from the ground up for the low-resource environments where maternal mortality is highest.

KHEM has been workshopped with community health volunteers in Turkana, Tana River, and Elgeyo Marakwet — three of Kenya's most underserved counties — who validated the data against local knowledge and identified unmapped facilities for OpenStreetMap. This community-validated approach ensures the platform reflects ground truth, not just official records.

Award funding would enable three critical expansions: a dedicated Maternal Health Access overlay, community training workshops in five additional high-priority counties, and mobile-responsive offline optimisation for clinics without reliable internet. Full details are provided in the accompanying narrative.

The project is fully open-source under the MIT license, with comprehensive documentation, a contribution guide, and explicit privacy and SDG alignment statements. All code is available at https://github.com/geraldkombo/kenya-health-equity-map.

Thank you for considering this nomination. I welcome the opportunity to discuss how the Kenya Health Equity Map can contribute to your mission of advancing equitable maternal health care for all.
